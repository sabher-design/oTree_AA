Example 1:

In a group of six tournament participants, the final performances are as follows:

 Tournament participant  Gender Multiplier Number of correctly encoded words  Final performance 
 1 Woman 0.9   1  0.9
 2 Woman 0.9   2  1.8
 3 Woman 0.9   3  2.7
 4 Man 1   1  1
 5 Man 1   1  1
 6 Man 1   3  3

Question 1: Assuming that the special rule does not apply, who are the two winners?

Your answer is correct. The two tournament participants with the highest final performance (= number of correctly encoded words x multiplier) are the two winners of the tournament.

Question 2: Assuming that the special rule applies, who are the two winners?

Your answer is incorrect. Tournament participants 3 and 6 have the highest final performance (= number of correctly encoded words x multiplier) and are the winners of the tournament.

Since the special rule applies at least one winner must be a woman. This is the case for tournament participant 3. So the special rule is automatically fulfilled.
